CSE focuses on various aspects of computer technology, including programming, algorithms, data structures, operating systems, database systems, networking, artificial intelligence, and software engineering.
CSE graduates often pursue careers in software development, computer systems analysis, data science, cybersecurity, and research and development.


IT professionals work with computer networks, databases, software development, cybersecurity, and system administration.
They are responsible for managing information systems, ensuring data security, developing software applications, and providing technical support.
IT graduates can work in industries such as software development, IT consulting, data analysis.


ECE covers areas such as electronic circuits, digital and analog communications, microprocessors, signal processing, embedded systems, wireless communication, and network protocols.
ECE graduates find opportunities in industries related to telecommunications, consumer electronics, embedded systems, Internet of Things (IoT).

Mechanical Engineering involves the application of principles of physics and materials science to design, analyze, and manufacture mechanical systems.
MECH encompasses areas such as mechanics, thermodynamics, fluid mechanics, materials science, manufacturing processes, robotics, and control systems.
Mechanical engineers work on designing.
